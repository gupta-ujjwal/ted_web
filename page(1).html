Theme

Bridging the gaps

Sensing the need to break the walls between ideas and bring to light the issues which, in general consensus, can benefit the society, we came up with the theme 'Bridging the Gaps'. With this theme, we attempt to bridge the gap between reality and future. Weeding out the apocrypha, this event will serve as a benchmark to the emerging ideas and bridge any other gaps they come across, inspiring the society as a whole. At the event, we plan to bring together people and ideas, which one would not associate with each other on a regular day. But again, this is not just a regular day.

OUR TALKS

Health Neuroscience

Health Neuroscience is an interface between brain and physical health throughout the life-span. To determine between brain physiology, bidirectional and brain-behavior there are a few determinants and consequences of corporeal health. TEDxBMU provides an insight as to how brain connects to everything.

Material Science

Material Science is an essential component of forensic engineering which emphasizes the relationship between molecular and atomic structure of a material. The biggest question today in this field is the future of silicon. Is the supreme reign of silicon going to stay so for eternity or will it be taken over? TEDxBMU clears your doubts.

Psychology of Creativity

Psychology of Creativity is the relationship between creative process that goes on in mind and the complementary traits of a creative personality which is determined on the basis of convergent and divergent thinking. TEDxBMU bridges the gap between ability and reality to make use of the creative mind.

Selling and Persuasion

In today’s world millions of products are sold and bought over e-commerce sites and various platforms. People are getting more and more brand-conscious; there is an incessant desire for the brands our favourite celebrities wear or use. TEDx BMU bridges this gap between people and brands.

Politics

Politics, a topic of discussion which starts heated debates with everyone having their passionate view-points and opinions. But is politics more than just bureaucrats wearing fancy suits, and giving fake promises? Can it ensure a better future for all of us? TEDx BMU clears your doubts.

Smart Cities

They are sold to us as high-tech cities with smart solutions, water and power supply, e-connectivity, infrastructure and so on. But, is the government going overboard with their 100 smart cities plan? Will it be a money-sucking initiative, which looks good only on paper? TEDx BMU has answers.
